01 / Security Truths

Your Home Deadbolt is a Joke.

If you went to a big box hardware store, picked out a shiny deadbolt off the shelf, and installed it yourself, you are not buying security. You are buying the illusion of it. In the real world, a standard residential pin-and-tumbler lock is barely a speed bump for a determined intruder.

The 15-Second Bump Key

You don't need to be a master thief to get past a standard deadbolt. A technique called "lock bumping" takes mere seconds. A specially cut key is inserted into the lock, struck with a blunt object, and the kinetic energy jumps the pins right into the shear line. The door opens as if they had the original key. No forced entry, no broken glass.

"A heavy door means nothing if the lock inside it is hollow brass."

What Actually Works?

Real security requires high-security cylinders. Brands like Schlage Primus, Medeco, and Mul-T-Lock utilize advanced, pick-resistant technology. They incorporate side-bars, hardened steel inserts to prevent drilling, and complex pin arrays that completely neutralize bump keys.

Furthermore, true security relies on key control. If someone borrows your house key, can they just walk into a hardware store and copy it? High-security locks come with restricted keyways. It means only authorized locksmiths—like EZ Locksmith—can duplicate the key, and only when you present the proper authorization card.
